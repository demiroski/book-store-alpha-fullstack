To Kill a Mockingbird

About This Book

Author: Harper Lee  |  Genre: Literary Fiction, Historical, Social Drama

To Kill a Mockingbird is one of the most celebrated and impactful novels in American literature. Set in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama during the 1930s, the story is narrated by young Scout Finch, whose father, lawyer Atticus Finch, defends a Black man wrongly accused of a crime against a white woman.

Through Scout's innocent eyes, the novel exposes the deep-rooted racial injustice and moral complexity of Southern society. It is a story about empathy, courage, and the meaning of true justice — timeless themes that remain as relevant today as ever.

Winner of the Pulitzer Prize, this masterpiece is a powerful reminder that standing up for what is right takes courage, especially when the world around you is wrong.
