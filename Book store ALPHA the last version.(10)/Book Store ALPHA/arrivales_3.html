Radical Gardening

About This Book

Author: George McKay  |  Genre: Non-Fiction, Politics, Culture, Nature

Radical Gardening: Politics, Idealism and Rebellion in the Garden is a fascinating exploration of how gardening has been used throughout history as a form of protest, political idealism, and social rebellion. George McKay traces the surprising connections between gardens and revolutionary movements.

From guerrilla gardening in city streets to the wartime Dig for Victory campaigns, McKay shows how ordinary people have used the act of planting and growing as a powerful statement against authority, war, and inequality.

A thought-provoking and richly illustrated book, Radical Gardening invites readers to see the garden not just as a place of beauty, but as a space of resistance and imagination.
